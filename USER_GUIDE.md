# Menu/Tab Course Format - User Guide

## Introduction

The Menu/Tab course format displays your course in a beautiful grid layout on the main page, and organizes content within each section using tabs. This guide will show you how to create and manage tabs in your course sections.

## What Are Tabs?

Tabs allow you to organize activities and resources within a course section into separate, clickable tabs - similar to tabs in a web browser. This helps:

- **Reduce scrolling** - Keep related content together
- **Improve organization** - Group activities by topic or type
- **Enhance navigation** - Students can quickly find what they need
- **Create better structure** - Separate learning materials, assessments, and resources

### Example Course Structure

```
📚 Week 1: Introduction to Course
   ├── 📑 Tab: Learning Materials
   │   ├── 📄 Syllabus
   │   ├── 📹 Welcome Video
   │   └── 📖 Course Reading
   │
   ├── 📝 Tab: Activities
   │   ├── 💬 Discussion Forum
   │   └── ✍️ Reflection Assignment
   │
   └── 📊 Tab: Assessment
       └── 📝 Week 1 Quiz
```

## Before You Start

### Prerequisites

- You must have **editing rights** in the course (Teacher or Manager role)
- Your course must use the **Menu/Tab format**
- You should have some **activities or resources** ready to add

### Checking Your Course Format

1. Go to your course
2. Click the **⚙️ gear icon** (top right)
3. Select **"Edit settings"**
4. Under **"Course format"**, confirm it says **"Menu/Tab"**
5. If not, change it to "Menu/Tab" and save

---

## Creating Your First Tab

### Step 1: Turn On Editing

1. Navigate to your course
2. Click the **"Turn editing on"** button (top right corner)

### Step 2: Choose Your Section

1. Scroll to the section where you want to create tabs
2. Each section (Week 1, Topic 1, etc.) can have multiple tabs

### Step 3: Add a Subsection

1. Click the **⋮ (three dots)** menu in the section header
2. Select **"Add subsection"**

   ![Section Menu Location]
   ```
   Section 1: Week 1                    [⋮]
   ----------------------------------------
   - Assignment 1
   - Quiz 1
   ```

3. A form will appear

### Step 4: Name Your Tab

1. In the **"Subsection name"** field, enter your tab name
   - Example: "Learning Materials"
   - Example: "Assessment"
   - Example: "Additional Resources"

2. Click **"Save changes"**

### Step 5: View Your Tab

1. Click **"Turn editing off"** (top right)
2. Click on the section card to view the section
3. You should now see your tab at the top of the section!

## Adding Content to Tabs

### Adding Activities to a Tab

**When Editing is ON:**

1. Make sure editing is turned on
2. Go to your course section
3. You'll see your subsection displayed as an expandable section
4. Click **"Add an activity or resource"** inside the subsection
5. Choose your activity (Assignment, Quiz, Forum, etc.)
6. Configure and save the activity

**Visual Guide:**

```
📦 Section 1: Week 1
   ├── 📄 Assignment 1 (in main section)
   │
   └── 📂 Learning Materials (subsection/tab)
       └── ➕ Add an activity or resource
```

### Moving Existing Content to a Tab

You can move existing activities into your tabs:

1. Turn editing on
2. Find the activity you want to move
3. Click and hold the **✋ drag handle** (six dots) next to the activity
4. Drag it into the subsection (tab)
5. Release to drop it in the new location

**Alternative Method:**

1. Click the **⋮ menu** next to the activity
2. Select **"Move"**
3. Choose the subsection as the destination
4. Click **"Move here"**

## Creating Multiple Tabs

You can create as many tabs as you need within a section!

### Example: Three-Tab Structure

**Step 1: Create First Tab**
1. Add subsection named "Introduction"

**Step 2: Create Second Tab**
1. Add another subsection named "Activities"

**Step 3: Create Third Tab**
1. Add another subsection named "Assessment"

  [Content shows here based on selected tab]

## Understanding the "Content" Tab

### What is the Content Tab?

If you add activities directly to a section (not in a subsection), they will automatically appear in a tab called **"Content"**.

### When to Use the Content Tab

**Use the Content tab for:**
- Quick announcements
- Section overview materials
- Resources that don't fit in other categories

**Example:**

```
Section 1: Week 1
├── 📢 Announcement (added to section directly)
├── 📅 Weekly Schedule (added to section directly)
│
├── Tab: "Content" (automatically created)
│   └── Shows the announcement and schedule
│
├── Tab: "Learning Materials" (subsection you created)
│   └── Your learning content
│
└── Tab: "Assessment" (subsection you created)
    └── Your quizzes and assignments
```

### Avoiding the Content Tab

If you don't want a "Content" tab:
- Put ALL activities in subsections
- Don't add any activities directly to the section
- Only add subsections to the section

## Managing Tabs

### Renaming a Tab

1. Turn editing on
2. Find the subsection (it appears as a section when editing)
3. Click the **✏️ Edit** icon next to the subsection name
4. Select **"Edit settings"**
5. Change the **"Subsection name"**
6. Click **"Save changes"**

### Reordering Tabs

Tabs appear in the order the subsections were created.

**To change tab order:**
1. Turn editing on
2. Use the **⬆️ ⬇️ arrows** next to the subsection
3. Or drag and drop using the **✋ drag handle**
4. Turn editing off to see the new tab order

### Hiding a Tab

1. Turn editing on
2. Click the **👁️ eye icon** next to the subsection
3. The eye will change to **👁️‍🗨️ (hidden)**
4. Students won't see this tab
5. Teachers still see it (dimmed)

**To show again:**
1. Click the **👁️‍🗨️ icon** again

### Deleting a Tab

⚠️ **Warning:** This deletes the tab AND all activities inside it!

1. Turn editing on
2. Click the **⋮ menu** next to the subsection
3. Select **"Delete"**
4. Confirm the deletion

**Tip:** Before deleting, move any activities you want to keep to another location.

## Best Practices

### 1. Plan Your Tab Structure

**Before creating tabs, consider:**
- What types of content do you have?
- How do students naturally group these materials?
- What makes logical sense for navigation?

**Good Tab Names:**
- ✅ "Week 1 Readings"
- ✅ "Practice Exercises"
- ✅ "Assessment"
- ✅ "Additional Resources"

**Avoid:**
- ❌ "Tab 1", "Tab 2" (not descriptive)
- ❌ Very long names (they may get truncated)
- ❌ Too many tabs (4-6 is usually enough)

### 2. Keep It Simple

**Recommended:**
- 3-5 tabs per section
- Clear, concise tab names
- Logical grouping of content

**Too many tabs can overwhelm students!**

### 3. Consistent Structure

Use the same tab structure across sections:

**Example: Consistent Weekly Structure**
```
Week 1:
├── Content
├── Learning Materials
├── Activities
└── Assessment

Week 2:
├── Content
├── Learning Materials
├── Activities
└── Assessment
```

Students will know what to expect each week!

### 4. Use the Content Tab Wisely

**Good uses:**
- Weekly announcements
- Overview or objectives
- Important notices

**Or don't use it at all:**
- Put everything in subsections
- More organized appearance

### 5. Mobile Consideration

Tabs work on mobile devices, but:
- Shorter tab names are better
- Fewer tabs = easier navigation
- Test your course on mobile

## Troubleshooting

### My tab isn't showing!

**Check:**
1. ✅ Did you turn editing off?
2. ✅ Is the subsection visible (not hidden)?
3. ✅ Did you clear your browser cache?
4. ✅ Are you viewing the section page (not the course home page)?

**Solution:**
- Click on the section card from the course home page
- Tabs only appear within individual sections

### I see "Subsection" instead of a tab

**When editing is ON:** Subsections appear as regular sections
**When editing is OFF:** Subsections appear as tabs

This is normal! Turn editing off to see tabs.

### The Content tab has activities I don't want

**Solution:**
Move those activities into subsections:
1. Turn editing on
2. Drag the activities into a subsection
3. Or delete them if not needed

### I can't add activities to my tab

**Check:**
1. Make sure editing is turned on
2. Click "Add an activity or resource" INSIDE the subsection
3. Make sure you have permission to add activities

### Tabs aren't in the right order

**Solution:**
1. Turn editing on
2. Reorder the subsections using drag-and-drop or arrows
3. Tab order follows subsection order

### Activities disappeared after deleting a tab

Unfortunately, deleting a subsection deletes its contents.

**Prevention:**
- Always move activities out before deleting
- Make backups of important courses

**Recovery:**
- Check your Moodle recycle bin (if enabled)
- Contact your site administrator

## Conclusion

Tabs in the Menu/Tab course format help you create organized, easy-to-navigate courses. By grouping related content together, you make it easier for students to find what they need and focus on learning.

**Key Takeaways:**
- ✅ Tabs are created using subsections
- ✅ Use 3-5 tabs per section for best results
- ✅ Keep tab names clear and concise
- ✅ Be consistent across your course
- ✅ Test from a student's perspective

Happy course building!
